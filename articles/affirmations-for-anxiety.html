25 Affirmations for Anxiety Relief, Backed by Therapists

When anxiety tightens its grip, the right words can help you loosen it. These affirmations are grounded in cognitive behavioral principles and recommended by licensed therapists.

Anxiety isn't just worry. It's a full-body experience: the tightening in your chest, the racing thoughts, the feeling that something is about to go wrong even when nothing is. In these moments, affirmations serve as a cognitive anchor, giving your mind something constructive to hold onto.

The affirmations below are organized by the type of anxiety they address. You don't need to use all 25. Find the ones that speak to where you are right now.

When you feel overwhelmed

  1. I can only control what I can control, and I release the rest.
  2. This feeling is temporary. It has always passed before.
  3. I don't need to solve everything right now.
  4. I am allowed to take things one step at a time.
  5. My pace is valid, even when it feels slow.

When self-doubt creeps in

  1. I have handled difficult things before, and I can handle this.
  2. My worth is not measured by my productivity.
  3. I am more capable than my anxiety tells me I am.
  4. Making mistakes is part of being human, not proof that I'm failing.
  5. I don't need to have it all figured out to move forward.

When you're worried about the future

  1. I choose to focus on what I can do today.
  2. Uncertainty is not the same as danger.
  3. I trust my ability to navigate whatever comes.
  4. Not every thought I have is a fact.
  5. I can prepare without catastrophizing.

When you need to calm your body

  1. My body is safe right now.
  2. I breathe in calm and exhale tension.
  3. I give myself permission to rest.
  4. I don't have to carry this tension. I can set it down.
  5. My nervous system is doing its best to protect me, and I can gently reassure it.

When you feel alone in it

  1. Millions of people feel this way. I am not broken.
  2. Asking for help is a sign of strength, not weakness.
  3. I deserve support, and I am allowed to seek it.
  4. I am not a burden for having needs.
  5. I am worthy of the same compassion I give to others.

How to use these affirmations

Therapists recommend a few approaches:

  • In the moment: When anxiety spikes, choose one affirmation and repeat it slowly, three times. Pair it with deep breathing (inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 6).
  • As prevention: Read through 3-5 affirmations each morning. This builds a library of positive thought patterns that your brain can access more easily when stress hits.
  • As journaling prompts: Write an affirmation at the top of a journal page and spend five minutes exploring what it means to you personally.

Remember: affirmations are not a replacement for professional support. If anxiety is significantly impacting your daily life, please reach out to a licensed therapist or counselor.
